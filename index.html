Home Assistant

Home Assistant as Experience Sampling Tool

The Experience Sampling Method or Ecological Momentary Assessments (EMA) is a research methodology involving the capture of self-reported participant data in longitudinal field studies. This kind of information is valuable to directly understand the participant’s state, and also to label ground truth for computational algorithms. The ubiquitous nature of smartphones has been heavily utilized in the past decade to conduct experience sampling, enabling a large growth in the use of experience sampling across multiple domains due to improved compliance by participants. However, we believe the use of a home assistant (such as Alexa or Google Home) as a medium for conducting experience sampling presents opportunities to collect even more rich and novel participant data. In our research, we developed a functional prototype of a voice assistant-based EMA platform, utilizing a Google Home and Google’s Dialogflow API. Further, we conducted a small-scale field study to test its effectiveness compared to the industry-standard methodology of typed smartphone responses. In our results, we found that our home assistant platform gathered rich user responses, without a tradeoff of more participant effort.

  • Date: December 2018
  • CS 7470: Mobile & Ubiquitous Computing
